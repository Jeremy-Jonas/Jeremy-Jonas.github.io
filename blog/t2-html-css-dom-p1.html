So what is HTML, CSS and the DOM?

One way you could look at them is to liken them to specific types of people. HTML is the person who is all about reading the manual and following the instructions to the letter. HTML cares about the words on the page and where you go if you click something. CSS on the other hand is the person who is all about style, they love taking some standard text and adding colour, borders, adjusting the layout and style of the content and just generally making the place look good. CSS isn't really bothered about the words on the page or where you will end up if you click something. Finally, the DOM. DOM is like the person who makes a flowchart for everything, they thrive on visual representations of plain text instructions to clearly show the order of the process, and clearly show what is a sub-process of another.

Boxifying Design?

Boxifying (yep, it's a real word!) your design is the process of taking an image or concept drawing of what the website should look like, and then either drawing or cutting it into separate boxes for each part of the page. A quick example is if you have a page with a heading, an image and some text, you would put a box around each part and end up with 3 boxes.

The Box Model

As above, everything in web design ends up being a box. The best way to think of this is that there is an invisible grid on the webpage which the smaller boxes can align to. You can of course go deeper and imagine every box has an invisible grid of its own, and you can put as many boxes within a box as needed. This allows the developer to easily align and position elements on a webpage, and offers the added advantage that a total page layout overhaul can be done relatively quickly by simplay moving boxes around on the grid.
