Leadership

What is your experience of compassionate (or not) leadership?

I have experience both compassionate and non-compassionate leaders in my life, usually in the form of a team leader or a manager at a job. My experience with compassionate leaders was far more pleasant and resulted in a better work life for myself and the person leading me.

Do the multiplying goodness meditation. How did you find it?

Another interesting experience, and again not really sure I took as much away from the experience as the author of the book would have expected me to.

Do the Tonglen meditation. How did you find it?

I found it to be a similar experience to my experience with the multiplying goodness method above. I can see what the method is trying to accomplish from reading the summary in the book but my experience was really not anything like this description.

How does the SCARF model apply to your Phase 0 experience so far? How will it apply to bootcamp?

The SCARF model stands for Status, Certainty, Autonomy, Relatedness and Fairness. I think a lot of the principles within each haven't really applied to my study so far due to the fact that I have been working alone at home on it. I can see that the principles will become a lot more important when I begin bootcamp and am working as part of a team of my peers.

Have a difficult conversation. How did it go?

This blog was quite well timed actually as I did in fact need to have a difficult conversation on the day before writing it. I can't publish the specific details in this case but the outcome was one that was a slight compromise by both of us which we agreed upon after quite some time. Looking back I am satisfied with how the conversation went, everything that I needed to say was said and I believe the same to be true for the other person involved.
